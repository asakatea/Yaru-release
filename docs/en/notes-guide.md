# Notes

Yaru Notes is a wiki-style knowledge base where every idea connects to the next. Your notes live inside a **vault** -- a local, offline-first container that keeps everything organized and private.

## Vault and Note Tree

Each vault holds a tree of notes. You can nest notes under folders, drag them to reorder, and collapse branches you are not actively working on. Think of it like a personal wiki with a file-explorer sidebar.

## Daily Notes

Tap the calendar icon to create or open today's daily note. A new page is generated automatically with the current date as its title. Use daily notes for journaling, meeting minutes, or quick capture -- they slot neatly into the note tree by date.

## Wiki Links

Connect ideas by typing `[[` followed by the note title:

```
See also [[Linear Algebra Basics]] for the prerequisite material.
```

Yaru auto-completes matching titles as you type. When a linked note does not exist yet, tapping the link creates it instantly.

## Knowledge Graph

Open the graph view to see your vault visualized as an interactive network. Each node is a note; each edge is a wiki link. Zoom, pan, and tap any node to jump straight to that note. Clusters of densely linked notes reveal the natural structure of your knowledge.

## AI Chat Panel

Press the AI button inside any note to open a contextual chat panel. The assistant can summarize the current note, answer questions about its content, or suggest related topics. All AI traffic routes through Yaru's secure gateway -- your notes are never sent to third parties without your action.

## Block Palette

Use the **block palette** (tap `+` or type `/` on an empty line) to insert rich content blocks: headings, callouts, math equations, toggle lists, code blocks, and more. Each block type is designed to keep your notes expressive without leaving the editor.

## Creating Flashcards from Notes

Highlight any text and choose **Create Flashcard** from the context menu, or use the inline flashcard syntax directly in the editor (see the Editor Guide for syntax details). Cards are sent straight to your Memory decks for spaced-repetition review.

## Export and Import

Export individual notes or entire folders as Markdown, LaTeX, or PDF. Import `.md` files from other apps -- Yaru preserves front-matter and standard Markdown formatting during import.

## Search

The global search bar indexes every note in your vault by title and full text. Results appear instantly as you type, with matched keywords highlighted. Use filters to narrow results by folder, tag, or date range.
